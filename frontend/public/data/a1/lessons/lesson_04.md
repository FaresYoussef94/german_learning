## Lesson 4: Der Tisch ist schön!

### Furniture & Room Vocabulary (Möbel und Zimmer)
- **Furniture:** Bett (bed), Bild (picture), Gardine (curtain), Kommode (dresser), Lampe (lamp), Regal (shelf), Schrank (cabinet), Sessel (armchair), Sofa (sofa), Spiegel (mirror), Stuhl (chair), Teppich (carpet), Tisch (table)
- **Rooms:** Zimmer (room), Hotelzimmer (hotel room)
- **Other:** Möbel (furniture), Preis (price), Euro (euro), Designer (designer), Sonderangebot (special offer)

### Describing Objects with Adjectives
Adjectives used to describe furniture and rooms:
- **schön** — beautiful
- **hässlich** — ugly
- **(nicht mehr) modern** — (no longer) modern
- **praktisch** — practical
- **groß** — big
- **klein** — small
- **günstig** — cheap
- **teuer** — expensive
- **billig** — cheap/inexpensive

### Expressing Opinions
- **Ich finde [noun] schön.** — I find [noun] beautiful.
- **Das finde ich auch.** — I find that too.
- **Das finde ich nicht.** — I don't find that (beautiful/good).
- **Wie findest du...?** — How do you find...? / What do you think of...?

### Asking About and Stating Prices
- **Wie viel kostet der/die/das [noun]?** — How much does the [noun] cost?
- **[Noun] kostet [number] Euro.** — [Noun] costs [number] euros.
- **Das ist (sehr) teuer/günstig.** — That is (very) expensive/cheap.
- **Das ist aber sehr teuer!** — But that is very expensive!
- **Das ist ein Sonderangebot.** — That is a special offer.

### Numbers Continued (Hundreds and Thousands)
- **100:** (ein)hundert
- **200:** zweihundert
- **300:** dreihundert
- **351:** dreihunderteinundfünfzig
- **651:** sechshunderteinundfünfzig
- **1,000:** (ein)tausend
- **10,000:** zehntausend
- **100,000:** (ein)hunderttausend
- **1,000,000:** eine Million

**Note:** Numbers are written as one word in German: zweihundertsiebenundvierzigtausend (247,000)

### Currency Information
- **100 Cent = 1 Euro**
- **9,99 €** = "neun Euro neunundneunzig" (or informally: "neun Euro neunzig")
- **0,50 €** = "50 Cent"

### Grammar: Pronoun Reference (Nominative)
When referring to nouns by their gender:
- **der Tisch** → **er** (he/it)
- **das Bett** → **es** (it)
- **die Lampe** → **sie** (she/it)
- **die Tische** (plural) → **sie** (they)

Example: "Der Tisch kostet 1478 Euro. Er ist schön." (The table costs 1478 euros. It is beautiful.)

### Asking for Help
- **Brauchen Sie Hilfe?** — Do you need help? (formal)
- **Ja, bitte.** — Yes, please.
- **Nein, danke.** — No, thanks.

### Thank You & Please
- **danke** — thanks
- **bitte** — please
- **Vielen Dank!** — Thank you very much!
- **Bitte schön.** — You're welcome.

### Describing Hotel Rooms
- **Wie findest du Zimmer A?** — How do you like room A?
- **Ich finde Zimmer A schön. Das Bett ist modern und der Schrank ist praktisch.** — I find room A beautiful. The bed is modern and the wardrobe is practical.
- **Das finde ich auch.** — I agree.
- **Das finde ich nicht. Der Schrank in Zimmer A ist zu groß.** — I don't agree. The wardrobe in room A is too big.

### Sentence Structure with Adjectives
- Adjectives can come before nouns or after the verb **sein**
  - Before noun: **Das moderne Bett** (the modern bed)
  - After sein: **Das Bett ist modern.** (The bed is modern.)
