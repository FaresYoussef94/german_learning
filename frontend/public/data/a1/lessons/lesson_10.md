## Lesson 10: Ich steige jetzt in die U-Bahn ein.

### Overview
Lesson 10 introduces one of the most important grammar concepts in German: **separable verbs (trennbare Verben)**. The lesson is set at a train station and focuses on transportation vocabulary and how to use separable verbs to describe actions like boarding, arriving, departing, and changing trains. Understanding separable verbs is crucial for advanced German communication.

### SEPARABLE VERBS — The Core Grammar Concept

**What are separable verbs?**
Separable verbs (trennbare Verben) consist of two parts: a **prefix** (Präfix) and a **verb stem** (Verbstamm). The key characteristic is that in conjugated sentences, the prefix **separates** from the stem and moves to the **end of the sentence**.

**Structure: Prefix + Verb Stem**
- Examples: an|rufen, ein|steigen, aus|steigen, um|steigen, ein|kaufen, fern|sehen

#### How Separable Verbs Work

**In the INFINITIVE form (dictionary form):**
- The prefix and stem are together: **an-rufen** (to call), **ein-steigen** (to board)

**In PRESENT TENSE sentences:**
- The stem goes to **position 2** (conjugated)
- The prefix goes to **the end** of the sentence

**Example: an-rufen (to call)**

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Ich **rufe** dich **an**. | I call you. |
| Du **rufst** deine Mutter **an**. | You call your mother. |
| Er **ruft** seine Schwester **an**. | He calls his sister. |

**Example: ein-steigen (to board)**

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Ich **steige** in den Zug **ein**. | I board the train. |
| Wir **steigen** in die U-Bahn **ein**. | We board the subway. |
| Kannst du **einsteigen**? | Can you get in? |

**Example: ein-kaufen (to go shopping)**

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Ich **kaufe** im Supermarkt **ein**. | I shop at the supermarket. |
| Du **kaufst** Brötchen **ein**. | You buy bread rolls. |

#### Common Separable Verbs Used in Lesson 10

**an-rufen — to call (on phone)**
- Ich **rufe** deine Mutter **an**. (I call your mother.)

**an-kommen — to arrive**
- Um 09.45 Uhr **komme** ich am Hauptbahnhof **an**. (I arrive at the main station at 9:45.)

**ein-steigen — to board/get in**
- Ich **steige** jetzt in die U-Bahn **ein**. (I'm getting in the subway now.)

**aus-steigen — to exit/get off**
- Wann **steigst** du **aus**? (When are you getting off?)

**um-steigen — to change/transfer**
- Wo **steigen** wir **um**? (Where do we transfer?)

**ein-kaufen — to shop**
- Ich **kaufe** morgen Brötchen **ein**. (I'm shopping for bread rolls tomorrow.)

**fern-sehen — to watch TV**
- Heute Abend **sehen** wir **fern**. (We're watching TV this evening.)
- Kommst du auch? (Are you coming too?)

**mit-bringen — to bring along**
- Vielleicht **bringe** ich am Samstag meine Freundin **mit**. (Maybe I'll bring my girlfriend on Saturday.)

**ab-holen — to pick up**
- Kannst du mich am Hauptbahnhof **abholen**? (Can you pick me up at the main station?)

**ab-fahren — to depart**
- Der Zug **fährt** um 09.00 Uhr **ab**. (The train departs at 9:00.)

### Separable Verbs in Different Sentence Types

#### 1. Statements (Sätze)
Structure: **Subject + Conjugated Verb (Pos. 2) + ... + Prefix (Ende)**

- Ich **rufe** dich **an**. (I call you.)
- Du **steigst** in die U-Bahn **ein**. (You board the subway.)
- Er **kauft** im Supermarkt **ein**. (He shops at the supermarket.)

#### 2. Yes/No Questions (Ja/Nein-Fragen)
Structure: **Conjugated Verb + Subject + ... + Prefix**

- **Rufst** du dich **an**? (Do you call yourself?)
- **Steigst** du **ein**? (Are you getting in?)
- **Kaufst** du morgen **ein**? (Are you shopping tomorrow?)

#### 3. W-Questions (W-Fragen)
Structure: **W-Word + Conjugated Verb + Subject + ... + Prefix**

- Wann **rufst** du deine Mutter **an**? (When do you call your mother?)
- Wo **steigst** du **aus**? (Where are you getting off?)
- Wann **kommst** du **an**? (When are you arriving?)

### Sample Dialogue from Lesson 10

**Scene: Two friends at the train station**

**Ella:** Guten Morgen Karin, hier ist Ella. Wo bist du?

**Karin:** Hallo Ella! Ich steige gerade in den Zug ein. (I'm just boarding the train.)

**Ella:** Wann kommst du an? (When are you arriving?)

**Karin:** Um 09.35 Uhr am Ostbahnhof und um 09.45 Uhr am Hauptbahnhof.

**Ella:** Kannst du am Hauptbahnhof aussteigen? (Can you get off at the main station?)

**Karin:** Ich könnte dich abholen. (I could pick you up.) Jetzt kaufe ich noch Brötchen ein, dann können wir zusammen frühstücken.

**Ella:** Gute Idee. Also dann, bis bald. (Good idea. See you soon.)

### Grammar: Present Perfect with Separable Verbs

In present perfect, separable verbs combine as one word with the past participle:

- **angerufen** → Ich habe dich angerufen. (I called you.)
- **angekommen** → Sie ist pünktlich angekommen. (She arrived on time.)
- **eingekauft** → Wir haben eingekauft. (We went shopping.)
- **ferngesehen** → Ihr habt ferngesehen. (You watched TV.)

### Important: Separable vs. Inseparable Verbs

**Separable verbs** (prefix separates in present tense):
- **an**-kommen → Ich **komme** **an** (I arrive)
- **ein**-kaufen → Ich **kaufe** **ein** (I shop)

**Inseparable verbs** (prefix stays attached):
- **be**kommen → Ich **bekomme** (I receive)
- **ver**stehen → Ich **verstehe** (I understand)

### Critical Grammar Points

**The separable verb rule:**
1. In infinitive: prefix + stem
2. In present tense: prefix moves to END
3. In questions: prefix still goes to END
4. In present perfect: prefix and stem combine into one word with "ge"

**Position table:**

| Sentence Type | Position 2 | Position 3-N | Ende (End) |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Statement | Conjugated stem | ... | Prefix |
| Yes/No Question | Conjugated stem | Subject ... | Prefix |
| W-Question | W-word, Verb | Subject ... | Prefix |

### Key Takeaway for Lesson 10

**Separable verbs are essential to German.** They're extremely common and must be used correctly in order to form proper sentences. The key is remembering that the **prefix separates and goes to the end** in present tense sentences, but **reunites with the stem** in infinitive and present perfect forms.
