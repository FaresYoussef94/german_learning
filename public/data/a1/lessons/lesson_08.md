## Lesson 8: Kein Problem. Ich habe Zeit!

### Overview
Lesson 8 focuses on making plans, telling time, scheduling activities, and discussing leisure activities. The main themes are time (both informal and formal), days of the week, and social planning with friends. This lesson builds on Lesson 6's understanding of verb cases and introduces more complex time-telling vocabulary.

### Places & Venues (Orte & Veranstaltungen)

Common places to go for activities and entertainment:

- **Restaurant** (das) — restaurant, dining establishment
- **Café** (das) — café, coffee shop
- **Ausstellung** (die) — exhibition, art exhibition
- **Museum** (das) — museum
- **Bar** (die) — bar, pub
- **Schwimmbad** (das) — swimming pool
- **Kino** (das) — cinema, movie theater
- **Konzert** (das) — concert
- **Disco** (die) — disco, nightclub
- **Kneipe** (die) — pub, tavern (informal)
- **Theater** (das) — theater

### Days of the Week (Wochentage)

The German week has seven days. Days are masculine (der) and are always capitalized:

- **Montag** — Monday
- **Dienstag** — Tuesday
- **Mittwoch** — Wednesday (literally "mid-week")
- **Donnerstag** — Thursday
- **Freitag** — Friday
- **Samstag** — Saturday (also called Sonnabend in some regions)
- **Sonntag** — Sunday

**Weekend:** Das Wochenende includes **Samstag** and **Sonntag** (Saturday and Sunday).

**Using days in sentences:**
- Ich gehe **am Montag** ins Konzert. (I'm going to the concert on Monday.)
- **Am Wochenende** habe ich Zeit. (I have time on the weekend.)

### Times of Day (Tageszeiten)

German divides the day into specific time periods:

| German | English | Time Frame |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **am Morgen** | in the morning | early morning (before 10 AM) |
| **am Vormittag** | late morning | 10 AM - 12 PM |
| **am Mittag** | at midday/noon | around 12 PM |
| **am Nachmittag** | in the afternoon | 12 PM - 5 PM |
| **am Abend** | in the evening | 5 PM - 10 PM |
| **in der Nacht** | at night/during the night | 10 PM - 6 AM |

**Examples:**
- Heute **am Nachmittag** gehe ich ins Museum. (This afternoon I'm going to the museum.)
- Ich habe **am Wochenende** Zeit. (I have time on the weekend.)
- Der Kurs beginnt **um neun Abends**. (The course starts at 9 PM.)

### Telling Time: Informal Style (Informelle Uhrzeit)

Germans use two systems for telling time. The informal system is used in conversation and everyday speech.

**The Informal Clock (12-hour system):**

The informal system divides the hour into quarters and uses "vor" (before) and "nach" (after):

| Expression | Meaning | Time |
|------------|---------|------|
| Es ist ... | It is ... | — |
| ... vor ... | ... (minutes) before ... | refers to the next hour |
| ... nach ... | ... (minutes) after ... | refers to the current hour |
| Viertel vor ... | quarter before ... | 15 minutes before the hour |
| Viertel nach ... | quarter after ... | 15 minutes after the hour |
| Halb ... | half ... | 30 minutes (half hour) |

**Key points about the informal system:**

1. **Minutes after the hour:** Use "nach" (after)
   - 3:05 = Es ist fünf **nach** drei. (It's five after three.)
   - 3:10 = Es ist zehn **nach** drei. (It's ten after three.)

2. **Quarter and half hour:**
   - 3:15 = Es ist Viertel **nach** drei. (It's quarter after three.)
   - 3:30 = Es ist halb vier. (It's half four — NOTE: Germans say "half" of the NEXT hour!)
   - 3:45 = Es ist Viertel **vor** vier. (It's quarter before four.)

3. **Minutes before the hour:** Use "vor" (before)
   - 3:50 = Es ist zehn **vor** vier. (It's ten before four.)
   - 3:55 = Es ist fünf **vor** vier. (It's five before four.)

**Important:** When saying 3:30, Germans say **"halb vier"** (half four), NOT "halb drei." The "halb" always refers to the NEXT hour.

**Conversation examples:**
- **Hast du heute Nachmittag Zeit?** (Do you have time this afternoon?) — Nein, leider nicht. (No, unfortunately not.)
- **Warum nicht?** (Why not?) — Heute Nachmittag gehe ich ins Museum. (This afternoon I'm going to the museum.)

### Telling Time: Formal Style (Formelle Uhrzeit)

The formal system uses 24-hour clock notation and is used in official contexts (announcements, schedules, radio/TV).

**The 24-hour system:**

| Time | Informal | Formal |
|------|----------|--------|
| 7:45 | Viertel vor acht | sieben Uhr fünfundvierzig |
| 10:50 | zehn vor elf | zehn Uhr fünfzig |
| 3:15 | Viertel nach drei | fünfzehn Uhr fünfzehn |
| 8:25 | fünf vor halb neun | zwanzig Uhr fünfundzwanzig |
| 11:45 | Viertel vor zwölf | elf Uhr fünfundvierzig |
| 7:05 | fünf nach sieben | sieben Uhr fünf |
| 4:50 | zehn vor vier | sechzehn Uhr fünfzig |

**Pattern for formal time:**
- State the hour (Stunden) first
- Then state the minutes (Minuten)
- Example: **19:25** = neunzehn Uhr fünfundzwanzig (7:25 PM)

**Examples in context:**
- Der Kurs beginnt **um neun Abends / um einundzwanzig Uhr.** (The course begins at 9 PM / at 21:00.)
- Im Radio sagen sie: "Es ist **sieben Uhr fünfundvierzig.**" (On the radio they say: "It is 7:45.")

### Making Plans & Scheduling (Terminabsprachen)

**Key verbs:**
- **gehen** (to go) — used with locations: ins Kino gehen, ins Museum gehen
- **haben** (to have) — to have time: Ich habe Zeit. (I have time.)
- **können** (can/to be able) — modal verb: Ich kann heute nicht kommen. (I can't come today.)

**Useful expressions:**

| German | English |
|--------|---------|
| Hast du heute Nachmittag Zeit? | Do you have time this afternoon? |
| Nein, leider nicht. | No, unfortunately not. |
| Warum nicht? | Why not? |
| Ich gehe ins Kino. | I'm going to the cinema. |
| Ich gehe ins Museum. | I'm going to the museum. |
| Am Wochenende habe ich Zeit. | I have time on the weekend. |
| Du auch? | You too? |
| Können wir uns treffen? | Can we meet? |

### Sample Dialogue

**Clara:** Hallo Manuel! Können wir heute Nachmittag zusammen ausgehen?
(Hello Manuel! Can we go out together this afternoon?)

**Manuel:** Leider nicht. Ich habe noch einen Termin mit meiner Chefin. Das tut mir sehr leid.
(Unfortunately not. I still have an appointment with my boss. I'm very sorry.)

**Clara:** Schade! Warum nicht?
(Too bad! Why not?)

**Manuel:** Am Wochenende habe ich Zeit. Du auch?
(I have time on the weekend. You too?)

**Clara:** Ja, super! Wann treffen wir uns?
(Yes, great! When shall we meet?)

**Manuel:** Samstag am Nachmittag? Vielleicht ins Schwimmbad?
(Saturday afternoon? Maybe to the swimming pool?)

**Clara:** Sehr gerne! Viertel nach zwei?
(I'd like that! Quarter after two?)

**Manuel:** Perfekt! Bis Samstag!
(Perfect! See you Saturday!)

### Grammar Focus: Time Expressions with "am" and "um"

**Using "am" (at the [time of day]):**
- **am Montag** — on Monday
- **am Morgen** — in the morning
- **am Nachmittag** — in the afternoon
- **am Wochenende** — on the weekend

**Using "um" (at [specific time]):**
- **um 3 Uhr** — at 3 o'clock
- **um halb vier** — at 3:30
- **um Viertel nach zwei** — at 2:15
- **um neun Abends** — at 9 PM

**Combining both:**
- Ich gehe **am Freitag um 7 Uhr** ins Theater.
(I'm going to the theater on Friday at 7 o'clock.)

### Grammar Focus: "Ins" and "In die"

Lesson 8 introduces the contraction **"ins"** (in + das):

- Ich gehe **ins** Kino. (I'm going into the cinema. — neuter)
- Ich gehe **in die** Bar. (I'm going into the bar. — feminine)
- Ich gehe **in die** Ausstellung. (I'm going into the exhibition. — feminine)

This uses the accusative case (destination/direction), introduced in Lesson 6.

### Preposition "in der" (in the — locative)

For stating WHERE you are (not going), use "in der" (dative case):
- Ich bin **in der** Bar. (I am in the bar.)
- Wir treffen uns **in der** Disco. (We meet in the disco.)

**Verbs related to this lesson:**

### Important Notes & Exceptions

1. **German uses 24-hour clock in formal contexts:** TV schedules, train times, official announcements use 19:30 rather than 7:30 PM.

2. **"Halb" always refers to the next hour:** 3:30 = "halb vier" (half four), NOT "halb drei."

3. **Saturday variations:** "Samstag" is used in most of Germany, but "Sonnabend" is used in some northern regions. Both are correct.

4. **Weekend is singular in German:** "das Wochenende" (singular), though it encompasses two days.

5. **"Nachts" (at night) can also be used:** "Ich arbeite nachts." (I work at night.) This is more general than "in der Nacht."

6. **Regional time systems:** Some regions use different terminology, but the formal 24-hour system is universal and always understood.
